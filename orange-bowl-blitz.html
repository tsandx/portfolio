Orange Bowl Blitz

A celebration of UF's off-the-field wins — combining digital display trucks, real-time fan UGC, and a hashtag blitz across Miami to own the narrative when #7 UF met fellow elite public university Virginia in the Orange Bowl.

Role   Director of Social Media
Platforms   Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Giphy, OOH
Org   University of Florida

The goal

Celebrate UF's academic rise — #7 among national public universities, up from #19 in 2012 — at the exact moment Gator Nation had a national stage. The Orange Bowl wasn't just a football matchup: UF was facing the University of Virginia, itself a highly ranked US News institution. The goal was to own both the cultural moment and the academic narrative, using Miami as the canvas.

The spark

When UF received its Orange Bowl selection in December 2019, the art world was buzzing about Maurizio Cattelan's "Comedian" — a banana duct-taped to a wall that had just sold for $120,000. The response was instant: photograph an orange, tape it to a wall, and post it. The internet got it immediately.

2.9K likes  ·  726 retweets  ·  The tweet that kicked it all off

The campaign

From that tweet, the campaign expanded into a fully integrated blitz. A hype video — "The Rise Continues" — dramatized UF's climb from #19 to #7 and earned 2.1K likes and 683 shares on Facebook alone. Five custom "Every Day Is Game Day" billboard designs ran across Miami's major transportation corridors, reinforcing the academic achievement message alongside the bowl hype.

LED-equipped box trucks drove continuous loops through Hard Rock Stadium, Wynwood, Downtown Miami, and South Beach — displaying a live, real-time feed of #GoGators fan UGC pulled from around the city. Fans who posted with the hashtag saw their photos appear on the trucks. The city became the content.

On the streets of Miami

Game day

On game day the social team was on the ground at Hard Rock Stadium — photographing fans, capturing moments, and publishing in real time. A 115-photo Facebook gallery and a stream of TikTok clips, Instagram Stories, and tweets brought Gator Nation along for every quarter. The game itself was a fitting close to the campaign: UF beat Virginia 36–28. The final tweet: "Started with a win against Miami. Ended with a win in Miami. It's great to be a FLORIDA Gator!"

The win

In just over 24 hours — not counting impressions from LED trucks, digital billboards, and OOH placements across Miami:

1.1M Twitter impressions
678K Facebook people reached
135K Instagram video views
500K Giphy GIF views

The campaign proved that social media's job isn't just to cover the game — it's to shape the story around it. By centering UF's academic achievement instead of just football, the Orange Bowl Blitz extended the university's brand far beyond sports fans and made a case for what a top-10 public university looks like when it shows up.
