I fell in love with the internet before most people knew it existed, and with social media before it had a job title. By the time it did, I'd already been building communities online for years — first as a webmaster, then as the person who kept asking "what if we tried this?" until someone made it my actual job.

I was the inaugural Director of Social Media at two universities — University of Wisconsin–Green Bay and the University of Florida. At UF, I built a social presence that grew from 500K to over 2 million followers, earned coverage in Wired and The Chronicle of Higher Education, and produced work that ended up in a CDC study and on Giphy's all-time leaderboard above Google, Nike, and Facebook.

I also taught. For three years I was an adjunct lecturer at UF's College of Journalism — teaching the university's first course on storytelling for the social web. The best campaigns I ran came from the same instinct I brought to the classroom: curiosity, trust in the community, and a bias toward making something real rather than something safe.

I'm based in Menlo Park. I moved to the Bay Area because I wanted to be close to what's next — the same reason I joined social media in 2008.
