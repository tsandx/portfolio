I wasn't sure what to put here. You already have my resume and I don't want to waste your time with redundant stuff. What I'd like is for you to get an idea if I'd be fit for your team, or more to the point, if we'd be miserable together.

There's a type of social media professional I'm not: the one who mistakes a beautiful Instagram grid for a strategy. Brands that live strictly inside their style guide are usually invisible to the people they're trying to reach. Social media moves faster than any marketing department can document. Real-time content requires real-time judgment — the ability to understand a brand so deeply that your voice becomes its voice. I do push limits, but at my age I've learned to avoid injury by bending not breaking. Placing the brand above the engagement-seeking noise, but below the community it supports. Social has the potential to be beautiful, but a brand can't stare at the mirror waiting for it to happen.

I love analytics, especially real-time data for agility and benchmarking for growth. But I also trust intuition and the magic of the unexpected. TikTok is a perfect example — we were there before our community knew it existed. The early numbers said abandon it. The gut said give it time. We stayed, we learned, we became the university others were chasing. You can't buy what we were able to earn.
