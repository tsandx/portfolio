Things I will do:

Ask the president of the university to act sad and eat a sandwich on a random bench on campus. Why? To show the students on spring break that their president misses them on campus.

Let numbers motivate not dictate

I love analytics, especially real-time data for agility and benchmarking for growth. But I also trust intuition and the magic of the unexpected. TikTok is a perfect example — we were there before our community knew it existed. The early numbers said abandon it. The gut said give it time. We stayed, we learned, we became the university others were chasing. You can't buy the social street cred we were able to gain... priceless and powerful.

My mantra... a philosophy? Nah, just vibes.

If you're not having fun cooking it, don't expect others to enjoy eating it.
Do the job right and your community should make the job obsolete.
Social doesn't sleep. Opportunities are born at night and on weekends.
Show and tell doesn't always make the audience think and feel.
People are awesome, people are assholes. Don't water the weeds.
