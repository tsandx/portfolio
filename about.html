I didn't wait for social media to become a career. I just started.

I was the inaugural Director of Social Media at two universities — University of Wisconsin–Green Bay and the University of Florida. At UF, I built a social presence that quadrupled the university's followers, earned coverage in Wired and The Chronicle of Higher Education, and produced work that ended up in a CDC study and outpaced Nike on Giphy.

I taught as an adjunct lecturer at UF's College of Journalism — creating the university's first course on storytelling for the social web. The best campaigns I ran came from the same instinct I brought to the classroom: curiosity, trust in the community, and a bias toward making something real rather than something safe.

I'm based in Menlo Park, California.

Who I am — and who I'm not

There's a type of social media professional I'm not: the one who mistakes a beautiful Instagram grid for a strategy. Brands that live strictly inside their style guide are usually invisible to the people they're trying to reach. Social media moves faster than any marketing department can document. Real-time content requires real-time judgment — the ability to understand a brand so deeply that your voice becomes its voice, while still serving the audience first. I've never been consumed with faking the perfect appearance on social. The real work goes much deeper than making people see you — it makes them think and feel.

I love analytics, especially real-time data. But I also trust intuition and trying things others won't. TikTok is a perfect example — we were there so early we were alone. The numbers said abandon it. The gut said this is likely to be the place to be. We stayed, and became the first verified university on the platform.

Philosophy

"If you're not having fun making the meal, don't expect others to enjoy eating it."

"If you do the job right, your community will put you out of work with their brilliant content and authentic voice."

"Social doesn't sleep. The opportunities live after your competition signs off for the weekend."
