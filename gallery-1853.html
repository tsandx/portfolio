Campaign · University of Florida · November 2016

Gallery 1853

A social media photography contest that became a real art show, a coffee table book, and permanent installation in the President's Office — entirely curated by the UF community.

Role   Director of Social Media
Platform   Instagram, Twitter + on-campus
Org   University of Florida

The goal

Prove that a hashtag could do more than generate content — it could curate an art collection. The idea was to invite the entire UF community to see campus through a photographer's eye, then take the best of what they captured and give it a physical life worthy of the institution.

How it worked

01
The call for submissions
UF students, faculty, staff and alumni were invited to post their best Gator-centric photography to Instagram or Twitter using #Gallery1853. The contest ran across five categories, each capturing a different facet of life at UF.
02
300+ submissions
The community responded. Over 300 photos were submitted across five categories — a genuine cross-section of campus life as seen through the eyes of the people who live it.
03
The gallery show on Union Green
The UF Social Street Team curated five finalists per category — 25 total — and mounted them in a pop-up art gallery outside the Reitz Union on November 16–17, 2016. The community voted in person for the winner in each category.
04
The coffee table book
All 25 finalist photos were compiled into a coffee table book gifted to a select group of major donors — a tangible artifact of the community's creativity presented at the highest level of the institution.
05
Permanent display in the President's Office
The five category winners — chosen by community vote — were framed and hung permanently in the Office of the President. A hashtag became institutional art.

Five categories

That's So Gainesville
Nature on Campus
Campus Sunrises / Sunsets
School Spirit
Passion Project

The gallery show

On November 16 and 17, 2016, the 25 finalist prints were displayed on tables across Union Green. Passersby stopped to study the work, cast ballots, take selfies with their favorites — and in some cases, discover their own photo on display for the first time.

The coffee table book

All 25 finalist images — with photographer credits pulled directly from their Instagram and Twitter posts — were printed in a Gallery 1853 coffee table book. The book was gifted to major donors as a token of the community's creative spirit and a reflection of what UF looks like through the eyes of those who belong to it.

The President's Office

The five community-voted winners were professionally framed and hung in the Office of the President — alongside the wall text explaining that every image began as a hashtag. A social media campaign had produced work deemed worthy of the most prominent space on campus.

The win

300+ photo submissions via #Gallery1853
25 finalists exhibited in a real art show
5 winners displayed permanently in the President's Office

Gallery 1853 showed what social media can be when it's treated as a creative platform rather than a broadcast channel. The community didn't just engage — they made something worth hanging on a wall.
